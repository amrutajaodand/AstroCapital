AstroHack Week 2018

We have a fun AstroHackWeek here at Leiden during the early August 2018. This is a week-long unconference and hackathon which includes morning lectures and exercises (covering essential skills for working effectively with large astronomical datasets), and afternoon hack workshops and offers opportunities for collaborative research.

The group is very diverse: from astronomers to industry tech experts, from undergradute to Profs. This year have seen topics such as machine learning, Bayesian inference, and visualisations. Participants come here to learn, hack (this web is one the hack project!), and contribute various topics in astronomy research. Here are the highlights:

  • Check out the lecture notes on machine learning, deep learning, data visualisation, and more here
  • Various tutorials contributed by the participants, including samnpling techniques, bayesian inference, MCMC, Neural Networks, Learning how to code, Practice in machine learning
